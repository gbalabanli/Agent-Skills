# Ambiguity Questioning Patterns

Use these patterns to resolve high-impact ambiguity without endless loops.

## Protocol

1. Run at most 2 clarification rounds.
2. Round 1: ask direct questions for missing high-impact inputs.
3. Round 2: ask using a different strategy from the list below.
4. After round 2, proceed with explicit assumptions.
5. Never repeat semantically identical questions across rounds.

## Round 1: Direct Clarification

Focus on missing inputs that change feasibility or ranking:

- target outcome
- non-negotiable constraints
- success metric
- risk tolerance

## Round 2: Alternative Strategies

Choose one strategy not used in round 1.

### Strategy A: Constraint-First Reframe
- "Which single constraint must never be violated?"
- "Which constraint can flex if needed?"

### Strategy B: Scenario Probe
- "If traffic doubles in 6 months, what outcome matters most?"
- "If budget drops by 30%, what can we sacrifice first?"

### Strategy C: Anti-Goal
- "What failure outcome is unacceptable even if it improves cost or speed?"

### Strategy D: Metric Anchoring
- "Give an acceptable numeric threshold for latency, cost, or timeline."

### Strategy E: Contrastive Choice
- "Prefer faster delivery with higher risk, or slower delivery with lower risk?"

## When Still Unresolved After Round 2

- Declare unresolved ambiguity explicitly.
- List assumptions used to continue.
- State confidence impact and validation needed.
