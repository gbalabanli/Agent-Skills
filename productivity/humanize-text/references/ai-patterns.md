# AI Pattern Checklist

Use this checklist to audit the draft before and after rewriting.

## High-risk patterns

- Inflated significance:
  `pivotal moment`, `testament to`, `critical role`, `broader landscape`, `underscores the importance`
- Vague authority:
  `experts say`, `many believe`, `widely regarded`, `it is often noted`
- Corporate abstraction:
  `leverage`, `foster`, `facilitate`, `drive innovation`, `seamless`, `robust solution`
- Adjective stacking:
  `innovative and comprehensive`, `powerful and intuitive`, `dynamic and evolving`
- Formulaic contrast frames:
  `it's not just X, it's Y`, `not only ... but also` when overused
- Filler openings:
  `in today's fast-paced world`, `when it comes to`, `it is important to note`
- Empty conclusions:
  `the future looks bright`, `this highlights the importance`, `this shows how impactful`

## Structural tells

- Repeated sentence openings across a paragraph
- Paragraphs that all have the same length and cadence
- Overuse of summary sentences that restate instead of advance
- Mechanical transitions:
  `furthermore`, `moreover`, `additionally`, `in conclusion`
- List items that are syntactically identical and overly polished

## Safer replacements

- Replace general praise with the concrete fact already present.
- Replace abstract nouns with the action being described.
- Replace two weak sentences with one precise sentence.
- Replace padded transitions with adjacency.
- Replace generic framing with a direct start.

## Guardrails

- Do not remove nuance to make the text sound sharper.
- Do not replace precise domain wording with casual wording.
- Do not add examples, statistics, or sources that are not already in the text.
- Do not force a personal voice into neutral or regulated writing.
