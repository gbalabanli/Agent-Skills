# Rewrite Modes

Choose the mode that matches the user's intent and the genre.

## Light

Use when the draft is mostly usable and only needs de-robotizing.

- Keep sentence order mostly intact.
- Remove obvious filler and generic transitions.
- Tighten adjective stacks.
- Introduce small rhythm variation.
- Preserve formatting almost exactly.

## Standard

Use for most requests.

- Rewrite at the sentence level, not just by swapping vocabulary.
- Combine or split sentences to improve cadence.
- Replace generic framing with direct statements.
- Keep the same claims, structure, and overall length unless the source is bloated.

## Strong

Use when the draft sounds heavily synthetic or detector-flagged.

- Rebuild paragraphs for clarity and natural flow.
- Compress repetition aggressively.
- Reorder nearby sentences when that improves readability.
- Keep every material fact, qualifier, and constraint.
- Avoid turning the piece into a different genre.

## Genre adjustments

### Marketing
- Keep claims concrete and benefit-led.
- Remove hype before removing energy.
- Keep the CTA intact unless asked to change it.

### Email
- Prioritize brevity and natural phrasing.
- Remove performative enthusiasm unless the sender actually sounds that way.
- Keep asks and deadlines explicit.

### Technical
- Preserve terminology and precision.
- Humanize with cleaner flow, not with extra personality.
- Prefer shorter sentences and direct verbs.

### Essay or article
- Allow more cadence and voice.
- Keep transitions understated.
- Prefer specific observations over broad framing.

### Social
- Keep momentum and readability high.
- Cut list-like stiffness.
- Avoid trying to sound viral unless requested.
